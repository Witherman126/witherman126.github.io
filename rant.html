A rant of mine about the current state of the Internet


As of writing this (1st of August 2025, last updated 3rd of August 2025) privacy on the internet is at a huge risk. The reason being current laws in the United Kingdom and that are most likely coming soon to the US, Australia, Canada and countries that are part of the European Union.
I myself am a resident of one of the above.

The true intention and risks of these laws

The laws are about implementing verification of age for content on the internet that is meant for adults or can be "potentially harmful to children". The laws are being passed/have been passed as "Kids Online Safety Act" (KOSA, in the US), Online Safety Act (UK) and S-209 (Canada) to name some examples.
The way it is meant to be executed is via a system that can either estimate a user's age by submitting a face picture or by submitting a government issued ID.
While the cause may seem noble at first glance, this is merely a cover to the true intention that is de-anonymising the Internet as a whole. Not only that - this is a security risk, as companies tasked with implementing verification cannot be trusted - one of them has already suffered a data breach. I am not naming which one as to not shame them - this is just a proof of concept that it is a risk.
Crushing privacy on the internet is also not the only goal - a UK politician has blatantly stated that monitoring and censoring adult individuals on the internet is one of the prime objectives of the Online Safety Act.
So don't let anyone fool You - this IS about silencing free speech and mass surveillance of Internet Users.

How this probably affects you right now

1. If you use Twitter/X you may have stumbled upon a post being blocked, with the message "Due to local laws, we are temporarily restricting access to this content until X estimates your age." This has also been reported to happen to users situated outside of the US, EU member countries, UK, Canada or Australia.
2. Youtube is currently in the process of implementing verification for users using AI. Not to shame AI, but it has been proven to judge age wrong by an example below.
3. Discord has an age verification system for accessing NSFW channels, which can use your face to judge your age. There has already been a case where a user's account has been outright suspended and Discord support being unresponsive, because the AI has deemed the user to be under 13 years old (which violates Discord's TOS). To my knowledge the user was a young adult.
4. Allegedally Wikipedia has been hit by some form of censorship or some other form of limitation in the UK. I am yet to verify this, but there seem to be sources pointing out this is the case.

Suspicions and further ramifications

The whole thing came into force in a relatively short timespan and has been hidden pretty well until now - some may have already seen it coming, but the vast majority of Internet users did not. Theories about this being a coordinated effort are very popular right now and are absolutely plausible - getting even more likely each passing day. What has happened as of now is most likely just the beginning of a slippery slope into mass surveillance and a reality akin to Geore Orwell's "1984".
"Dystopian" would be a good word to describe the government's actions.
My idea is this: the governments of the world have realised that the Internet, being a source of near instant information about nearly anything is the biggest thing stopping them from being free to do anything they wish. Possibly stripping us of our own properties and rights, bringing the way we live as of now to the ground and setting up a completely authoritarian government that feeds us lies about itself and everything else, keeping us in a blissfull ignorance powered by deception and hate. Just as in the book "1984" I mentioned above.

Things I have noticed

Current actions to supress out speech and pricacy are very similar to what is currently the case in China, which only proves that this is a substantial risk as the idea is possible to implement. To be precise - in China this censorship has gone so far that citizens willingly self-censor themselves most of the time, the government's actions are completely unaccounted for, be it human rights violations, omitting potential obligations, propaganda in broad daylight and probably many more examples.

What I ask of You, the reader

Don't stay silent about it. Talk about it, keep the subject afloat so the idea of our privacy does not become forgotten. If You are able to, I encourage You to do something about it in a proactive fashion - contact your local representatives and explain your concerns about your right to online privacy. If there are any petitions against these laws in your area - sign one. Or two. Or however many you want. The governments need to know, that we are not going to stand for this.
And that we are not going down without a fight.


A note, which I could not fit anywhere else as of now: The UK petition against the Online Safety Act has reached over 400 thousand signatures. Despite that fact the UK Government has stated that they will not be repealing the act. Which says only one thing - if we wish to oppose the censorship then we will face heavy resistance, but we can push through.
Also, this rant really only covers the tip of the iceberg - there is way more to this.


Stay vigilant and thank you for your precious time.
